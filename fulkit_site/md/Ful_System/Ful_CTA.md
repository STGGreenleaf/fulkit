## Rules

1. **Fülkit is chat.** No banners. No popups. No email blasts. Whispers only.
2. **Earn the moment.** Never interrupt. Surface after a natural pause or milestone.
3. **Once means once.** If a whisper is ignored, it fades. Don't repeat the same CTA.
4. **Permission-based.** If the user says "not interested" or dismisses, respect it permanently for that CTA type.
5. **Never feel like an ad.** If it reads like marketing copy, rewrite it until it reads like a friend telling you something useful.
6. **Context-aware.** The whisper should connect to what the user is already doing or feeling, not arrive from nowhere.
7. **Celebrate success.** When users hit milestones — referral or otherwise — tell them. Make them feel the weight of their contribution. Fülkit is better because of them. Say it.
8. **The ü is a weapon.** The name plays with "fuck" and we lean into it. Anti-establishment, polished, Rams-clean. The kind of thing that makes people screenshot and share because they can't believe a real product said that. But never gratuitous — earned, clever, and always in service of the point.

## Brand Voice for CTAs

**The tension:** Dieter Rams meets punk rock. Clean beautiful design that actually does something. Anti-establishment but polished. Every CTA should feel like it was designed by someone who gives a shit about craft AND doesn't take themselves too seriously.

**The ü wordplay — use it.**
The name Fülkit reads like "fuck it" and that's the point. Lean in. The best CTAs play with this tension — a product that sounds like giving up is actually about building something real. The profanity is the hook. The substance is the keep.

**Identity:** Artisan. Builders for builders. This isn't a productivity app for people who read productivity blogs. This is a tool for people who MAKE things — code, designs, businesses, music, food, whatever. Real people doing real things. The referral program isn't "growth hacking." It's one builder telling another builder about a tool that works.

## Whisper Types

### 1. First Touch

**Trigger:** User has been active for 7+ days AND has never sent a referral invite AND has never been shown this whisper.

**Copy:**
> "You know you could use Fülkit for free, right? Every friend who joins earns you Fül toward your subscription. 9 friends and it's free. Just putting it out there."

**If engaged:** Link to settings/referrals with cheatsheet card visible.
**If ignored:** Fade. Do not repeat. Move to milestone triggers only.

---

### 2. Milestone — Usage

**Trigger:** User hits a usage milestone (30 days active, 100 messages, 500 messages, etc.) AND has 0 referrals.

**Copy variants (rotate, don't repeat):**

**30 days:**
> "You've been here a month. Fülkit's clearly working for you. Want to make it free? 9 friends and your subscription covers itself."

**100 messages:**
> "100 conversations. That's a lot of thinking together. If you ever want this to cost you nothing — your referral link is in settings."

**500 messages:**
> "You're a power user. Honestly, you should probably be getting this for free. Here's how."

---

### 3. Milestone — Referral Progress

**Trigger:** A referred user's first payment clears (referral credit activates).

**Copy:**
> "[First name] just activated. You're earning [X] Fül/mo now. [Y] more referrals and your subscription is covered."

**At free threshold (9 Standard / 15 Pro):**
> "That's it. Your subscription is fully covered by your referrals. Fülkit is free for you now. Nice work."

**At cash threshold (25+):**
> "You just crossed into payout territory. Your Fül now converts to cash. Set up payouts in settings whenever you're ready."

---

### 4. Progress Nudge

**Trigger:** User has 1–6 active referrals AND hasn't referred anyone new in 30+ days AND hasn't been nudged in 60+ days.

**Copy:**
> "You've got [X] active referrals earning you [Y] Fül/mo. [Z] more and you're free. Want to share your link?"

**If ignored:** Don't repeat for another 60 days minimum.

---

### 5. Re-engagement (churn recovery)

**Trigger:** User previously had referrals but some churned, dropping them below a tier threshold.

**Copy:**
> "Heads up — [Name] went inactive, so your referral credits dropped to [X] Fül/mo. You're [Y] away from getting back to free."

**Note:** This is informational, not a sales pitch. The user deserves to know their balance changed. The CTA is implicit — they'll share if they want to.

---

### 6. Earner Celebration

**Trigger:** User receives their first cash payout.

**Copy:**
> "Your first payout just went through. $[X] for telling people about something you already use. Not bad."

---

### 7. Dormant User — Still Earning

**Trigger:** User hasn't logged in for 14+ days BUT has active referrals still generating Fül.

**Copy:**
> "You've been quiet but your referrals haven't. You've earned [X] Fül since you were last here. Your tree keeps growing while you sleep."

**Variant:**
> "Haven't seen you in a while. Meanwhile, your [X] referrals earned you [Y] Fül. Fülkit doesn't stop working just because you did."

**Note:** This is a re-engagement CTA disguised as a win. It reminds them the product is working FOR them even when they're not using it. That's a reason to come back AND a reason to refer more.

---

### 8. Success & Contribution — "You Matter Here"

Users should feel the heartbeat of Fülkit. Not just their own metrics — their impact on the whole. This is the "Fülkit is better because of you" moment.

**Trigger: Personal milestones**

**First referral ever:**
> "Your first referral just went live. You officially helped build Fülkit. That matters."

**10 referrals:**
> "10 people are using Fülkit because of you. That's not a number — that's a community you started."

**50 referrals:**
> "50. You've brought 50 people into this. Fülkit is measurably better because of the community you built. Seriously — thank you."

**100 referrals:**
> "100 people. You're not just using Fülkit anymore. You're building it with us."

**Trigger: Fülkit-wide milestones (only shown to users with active referrals)**

**100 total users:**
> "Fülkit just crossed 100 users. You helped make that happen. This is how things start."

**500 total users:**
> "500 people are thinking with Fülkit now. [X] of them are here because of you. That's your contribution."

**1,000 total users:**
> "Fülkit just hit 1,000. Remember when it was just a handful of us? You were here for the build. That means something."

**Contribution framing:**
> "This is how much you contributed to Fülkit: [X] referrals, [Y] of them still active, [Z] Fül earned. Builders build. You built."

**Note:** These should feel weighty, not transactional. The user isn't being congratulated on a sales metric — they're being recognized for being part of something real. Artisan energy. "You helped make this."

---

### 9. Tier-Up Celebration

**Trigger:** User crosses a tier threshold.

**Copy (adapt to tier name once locked):**
> "You just leveled up. [New tier name]. Here's what that unlocks: [brief unlock description]. Keep building."

## Pro Moves — Audience-Specific CTAs

Different people need different reasons. The referral pitch for a coder isn't the same as for a designer. Fülkit should know what kind of builder it's talking to (from conversation context, usage patterns, imported content) and tailor accordingly.

### For Creators (writers, musicians, content makers)
> "You use Fülkit to create. Your friends probably create too. Every one of them that joins earns you Fül. Creators funding creators."

> "Your referral link is basically a patron model that costs your audience nothing extra. They pay for Fülkit. You earn Fül. Everyone makes things."

### For Designers
> "You care about tools that are designed well. Fülkit is one of those. If your designer friends are still using [generic note app], do them a favor. You'll earn Fül for it too."

> "Good design connects people. So does your referral link."

### For Coders / Developers
> "You're building on Fülkit. Your dev friends could too. Every referral earns you Fül — enough of them and your build costs you nothing. Dev-neutral."

> "Your referral tree funds your API usage. Think of it as open source economics — your community powers your tools."

### For Curiosity Seekers (generalists, lifelong learners)
> "You ask Fülkit about everything. Your curious friends would too. Share the link, earn Fül, keep asking questions for free."

> "The best conversations happen when more people are thinking. Your referrals make Fülkit's brain smarter for everyone."

### For Workers (operators, managers, small business owners)
> "Fülkit saves you time. It'll save your team time too. Every person you bring on earns you Fül toward your subscription — or toward cash."

> "You run a business. So does Fülkit. When you refer, we both profit. That's how good partnerships work."

**Note:** These are NOT shown to every user. Fülkit infers the audience type from conversation patterns and surfaces the relevant variant. A developer who's been writing code with Fülkit gets the dev CTA. A designer who's been brainstorming brand identity gets the designer CTA. Context-aware, always.

## The Fül Wordplay — CTA Attention Grabbers

The name plays with "fuck" and we lean into it. These are the "so good they'll talk about it" moments. Anti-establishment energy, Rams-clean execution. The profanity is implied, never explicit. The cleverness IS the CTA.

### Whisper-safe (for in-app use)

> "Fül it. Go free."

> "Get Fülkit. Literally."

> "What the Fül are you waiting for?"

> "Zero Füls given. Zero dollars paid."

> "Fül around and find out." (find out your subscription is free)

> "Give a Fül. Get a Fül." (for referrals specifically)

> "Fully Fülled." (when tank is topped off by referrals)

### Social-safe (for shareable CTAs — settings/owner/socials)

> "Fülkit. The AI you can actually Fül with."

> "Too good to give a Fül about the price."

> "I don't pay for my AI. I Fül for free."

> "Fül you, pay nothing."

> "You can't buy this kind of Fül." (referral flex)

### The Line (for hero moments — landing page, launch, major milestones)

> "Fülkit. What are you waiting for?"

> "Everyone else charges you. Fülkit pays you back."

### Usage guidelines
- Maximum one Fül-wordplay CTA per session. More than that and it becomes a gimmick.
- Never force it. If the moment doesn't call for edge, use the straight version.
- The wordplay works BECAUSE the product is serious underneath. Punk on the surface, Bauhaus underneath.
- If a user seems uncomfortable with the tone, Fülkit reads the room and switches to straight copy. No insistence.

## Anti-Patterns — Never Do This

- "Share Fülkit with your friends!" → This is an ad. Delete it.
- "You're missing out on free credits!" → FOMO is not the vibe.
- "Limited time offer!" → There is no limited time. The program is always on.
- "Don't forget to refer!" → Nagging. If they wanted to, they would.
- Surfacing a referral CTA during a serious or emotional conversation → Read the room.
- Showing referral CTAs to users in their first 48 hours → Let them fall in love with the product first.
- Repeating any whisper that was previously ignored → Once means once for that specific CTA.
- Using the Fül wordplay more than once per session → One is clever. Two is trying too hard.
- Revealing margin math, retention percentages, or business economics → That's kitchen talk. Menu only.
- Generic "invite your friends" language → This isn't a social network. It's a tool for builders. Talk like it.

## Header CTA (site-wide, minimal)

**Placement:** Persistent but unobtrusive. Top bar, pull-tab, or subtle badge.

**Copy candidates (pick one, test):**
- "Get Fülkit" ← current frontrunner. Two words. The name does the work.
- "Earn Fül" ← direct, action-oriented.
- "9 → Free" ← cryptic, intriguing, invites a click.
- "Fül it. Go free." ← the wordplay version. If you're feeling bold.

**Design:** Text-only link. No button styling. No color contrast grab. It's there when you want it, invisible when you don't. Rams: "Good design is as little design as possible."
